Aniket Pramanik
PhD Candidate at CBIG Lab
I am a final year student working towards a PhD in Computational Imaging from Electrical and Computer Engineering, The University of Iowa, Iowa City, IA, USA.

Research Interests: Broadly, my research interest spans across Machine Learning, Deep Learning and Computational Imaging. I have gained experience in developing novel computational techniques for solving inverse imaging problems. Model-based image reconstruction using Deep Unrolled networks has been the hallmark of my research. My recent works are related to accelerated Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) acquisition and recovery. MRI is a non-invasive, non-ionizing imaging system providing excellent soft tissue contrast at minimal risk. The slowness of MRI makes high resolution imaging expensive and time-consuming. My work focusses on reconstruction of high quality images from partial scans to facilitate accelerated data acquisition with accurate downstream tasks like segmentation for diagnostic purposes.
